Subject: Chodesh Tov -- and what happened at a family birthday in Jerusalem

Wishing you a healthy and meaningful Rosh Chodesh Av.

This past week I performed at a private birthday dinner in Jerusalem -- a family gathering, a few dozen people, a restaurant back room. Not a huge production. Just one table at a time, up close.

What I love about those evenings is how quickly the walls come down. You start with a stranger across the table, someone who hasn't decided yet whether to trust you or play it cool -- and within a minute or two something shifts. A card lands in the wrong pocket. A word they were only thinking appears written on a slip of paper. And suddenly the whole table is in it together.

The host wrote to me afterwards: "I have to say, you really made the evening special."

That kind of event -- intimate, personal, nowhere to hide -- is where I do my best work.

If you have something coming up after Tisha B'Av, I'd love to be part of it. And if you know someone who's planning a birthday, a family reunion, or a company dinner, feel free to pass this along.
