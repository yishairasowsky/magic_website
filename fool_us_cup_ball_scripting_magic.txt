FOOL US - Cup and Ball (Scripting Magic version)
=================================================

FORMAT:
- SAY: = scripted lines
- DO:  = physical actions
- NOTE: = internal reminders

McCabe principles applied:
- Through-line established in first 30 seconds, paid off at the end
- No "watch," no "I'm going to," no narration of actions
- Lines get shorter and sparser as loads escalate -- let the objects do the work
- Spectator is engaged through curiosity, not instruction
- Ending answers the opening question; the lemon is not just a surprise, it's a revelation

------------------------------------------------------
THROUGH-LINE
------------------------------------------------------

"This ball has one habit: it disappears. I've spent years trying to figure out
where it goes. Tonight I think we find out."

The lemon is the answer. It always comes back -- and this time it brought
something with it.

------------------------------------------------------
SETUP (not said aloud)
------------------------------------------------------

In hands:
  Right - ordinary small foil ball
  Left  - cup (chop cup)

Right jacket pocket: Hecklau gimmick, giant foil ball (3rd big load)
Left jacket pocket:  lemon (4th load), magnetic small foil ball

Right pants pocket:  green tennis ball (1st big load)
Left pants pocket:   pink tennis ball (2nd big load)

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1: OPENING -- ESTABLISH THE QUESTION
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Walk out. Cup in left hand, foil ball in right. No introduction of props.

DO: Subtly show that the foil ball is not magnetic -- brush it past the cup
    or rest it against the rim. Don't announce this. Just let it register.

SAY: "This ball has one habit."

DO: Toss ball up once, catch it. Unhurried.

SAY: "It disappears."

DO: Place ball on left palm. Cover with cup. No fanfare.

DO: Pretend to take ball with right hand. Deposit into right jacket pocket.
    Ball stays in left hand under cup. Secure Hecklau gimmick with right fingers.

SAY: "I put it in my pocket."

DO: Mime flicking it back. Right hand (armed with gimmick) lifts cup. Ball
    sitting on left palm.

SAY: "It comes back."

DO: Look at the ball for a beat.

SAY: "Three years of this. I still don't know where it goes."

NOTE: Stop there. Don't elaborate. The audience now has a question -- where does
it go? -- and that question carries the entire routine. Everything that follows
is an attempt to answer it.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 2: THE CONVINCER -- DEEPEN THE MYSTERY
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Openly deposit ordinary ball into left jacket pocket. Immediately release
    it, grab magnetic ball instead. Remove, display in left hand.

SAY: "Let me try something."

DO: Cover ball with cup (right hand). Left hand mimes scooping ball from under
    cup, deposits (apparently) into left jacket pocket. Actually retrieves
    ordinary miniball from pocket instead.

SAY: "Gone."

DO: Produce miniball and show it. Cover it with cup. This time actually scoop
    it out with left hand. Open hand -- ball is there.

SAY: "Or not."

DO: Deposit ball into left pocket. Remove hand slowly. Open -- empty.

NOTE: No commentary. The audience feels the rhythm without narration. The ball
is making decisions of its own. That's the impression you're after.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 3: THE BANG -- THE QUESTION GETS HARDER
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Left hand open and flat. Right hand holds cup face-down above it.

SAY: "The question I keep asking myself -- where is it right now?"

DO: Bang cup sharply down onto left palm. Lift cup. Nothing.

SAY: "Not there."

DO: Apply pressure to dislodge chop ball from gimmick. Ball settles onto left
    palm under cup.

SAY: (to spectator, easy and unhurried) "Whenever you're ready, we'll begin."

DO: Lift cup. Magnetic ball sitting there on left palm.

SAY: "So. Let's start."

NOTE: "Whenever you're ready, we'll begin" implies nothing has happened yet --
which makes the reveal land harder. Underplay "So. Let's start." Don't punch it.
This is the peak of the solo phase; now we send the ball somewhere new.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 4: THE SPECTATOR -- SEND IT SOMEWHERE NEW
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Cover ball with cup.

SAY: "Every time it goes, it comes back to me. Let's see what happens if it
     goes to you instead."

DO: Mime sweeping ball from under cup with left hand. Pretend to deposit into
    left jacket pocket. Open left hand -- ball still there.

SAY: "I only pretend to put it in my pocket -- it's still in my hand."

DO: Cover ball with cup again. Left hand mimes scooping ball out from under cup,
    pretends to deposit into left jacket pocket -- actually grabs miniball from
    pocket instead. Bring out closed fist.

DO: Open left hand -- miniball is there. Ball is genuinely in hand.

SAY: (to spectator) "Open your hand -- cup it like a bowl."

DO: Place miniball on top of overturned cup in right hand. Tell spectator to
    open their hand palm up. Slap down with left hand on ordinary miniball.
    Simultaneously dislodge magnetic ball from gimmick.
    Magnetic ball drops into spectator's cupped hands. Left hand finger-palms
    ordinary ball. Cup repositions face-down.

SAY: (curious, not triumphant) "Did something land?"

NOTE: The spectator looks down and discovers the ball before you've announced
success. Better than asking "did it work?" -- that's self-congratulatory and
puts pressure on them to confirm your trick. This is genuinely curious.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 5: FIRST BIG LOAD -- GREEN TENNIS BALL
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Flourish cup one-handed to right-side-up. Something sounds inside. Let
    them hear it.

SAY: "Can I have that back?"

DO: Dump miniball from cup into spectator's hand. Right hand (holding green
    tennis ball in front of body to conceal it) brings hands together.
    Transfer cup from LEFT to RIGHT, loading green tennis ball. Relax right
    hand, swing slightly outward. Left hand takes miniball back from spectator.

DO: Glance at what's in their hand. Pause.

SAY: "That's not the same ball."

NOTE: Don't explain. Don't help them process. The observation is enough.

DO: Left hand places miniball on top of overturned cup (right hand, loaded).
    Open left hand takes it off, puts it in left pants pocket.
    Immediately grasp pink tennis ball.

SAY: "Open your hand."

DO: Release green tennis ball from cup. Show cup empty. Load pink. Transfer.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 6: SECOND BIG LOAD -- PINK TENNIS BALL
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Take green tennis ball back from spectator with right hand.

SAY: "This can't fit through the bottom."

DO: Show it. Set green into right jacket pocket. Grasp giant foil ball.

SAY: "Open your hand."

DO: Release pink from cup. Show empty. Load silver. Transfer.

NOTE: The patter is getting shorter. The objects are getting bigger. That's the
right proportion -- less talk, more impossible.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 7: THIRD BIG LOAD -- GIANT SILVER FOIL BALL
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Take pink tennis ball from spectator with left hand.

SAY: "Two won't fit."

DO: Show it. Put pink in left jacket pocket. Grasp lemon.

SAY: "The silver ball. I've lost track of it."

DO: Pause.

SAY: (to spectator) "Do you have it?"

DO: Relax right hand. Silver ball drops into spectator's open hand. Meanwhile
    remove lemon from left jacket pocket. Show cup empty at eye level.
    Transfer cup right to left, loading lemon securely.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 8: THE LEMON -- THE ANSWER
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Take silver ball from spectator with right hand.

SAY: "It always comes back."

DO: Beat. Look at the cup.

SAY: "I just never knew what it brought with it."

DO: Slowly open left hand. Overturn cup. Lemon falls out.

DO: Hold. Say nothing.

NOTE: The final line pays off the opening question -- "I still don't know where
it goes." Now we know: somewhere with lemons. The silence after the reveal is
part of the script. Don't fill it.

------------------------------------------------------
RUNNING TIME: approx. 5 minutes
------------------------------------------------------
