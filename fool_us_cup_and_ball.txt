FOOL US SUBMISSION - Cup and Ball (Chop Cup Routine)
=====================================================

FORMAT:
- SAY: = lines to speak
- DO:  = physical actions/moves
- NOTE: = internal notes/reminders

------------------------------------------------------
PREMISE
------------------------------------------------------

Penn and Teller have the most famous cups and balls act in magic. They do it with clear plastic cups so the audience can watch the balls appear and disappear in plain sight. It's a masterpiece.

This is the opposite of that.

One cup. One ball. No table. No assistants. Everything happens in my hands, surrounded, close enough to touch -- and the method is not what they think it is.

The routine builds through a series of impossible penetrations, then hands a ball directly to a spectator, and ends with a lemon appearing inside the cup.

The goal is to fool Penn and Teller by layering two separate gimmick systems (chop cup principle + Hecklau gimmick) in a way that makes each one cancel out the other as an explanation. When the chop cup explanation fits, the Hecklau gimmick is secretly doing the work -- and vice versa. The lemon load is the final impossibility: a cup clearly too small to contain it.

------------------------------------------------------
SETUP (not said aloud -- for submission purposes)
------------------------------------------------------

In hands:
  Right hand - ordinary small foil ball
  Left hand  - cup (chop cup with magnetic inner base)

Right jacket pocket: Hecklau gimmick, giant foil ball (3rd big load)
Left jacket pocket:  lemon (4th and final load), magnetic small foil ball

Right pants pocket:  green tennis ball (1st big load)
Left pants pocket:   pink tennis ball (2nd big load)

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 1: THE DISPLAY
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Walk out holding cup in left hand, small foil ball in right. No table. Just hands.

SAY: "One cup. One ball. I know what you're thinking -- this is the oldest trick in the world. And you're right. They found versions of this painted on Egyptian tomb walls. Roman poets complained about it. It's been around so long that at some point, everyone decided they already knew how it worked."

SAY: "I'm going to try to change your mind about that."

DO: Show both items cleanly. Toss the ball lightly in the air and catch it -- casual, unhurried.

SAY: "The ball goes in the cup."

DO: Place ball on left palm. Cover with cup.

SAY: "Except it doesn't."

DO: Pretend to take ball with right hand and deposit it into right jacket pocket. (Ball actually stays in left hand, hidden silently under the cup. Secure Hecklau gimmick with right fingers during pocket move.)

SAY: "The ball is in my pocket. I'm going to flick it back."

DO: Mime flicking ball from pocket toward cup. With right hand (armed with gimmick), lift cup to reveal ball resting on left palm.

NOTE: This is the first impossibility. The Hecklau gimmick is the method here -- not the chop cup -- but the chop cup is sitting there as a red herring.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 2: THE CONVINCER SEQUENCE
------------------------------------------------------

DO: With left hand, openly deposit ordinary small foil ball into left jacket pocket. Immediately release it and grab the magnetic small foil ball instead.

SAY: "Now let me show you something."

DO: Remove magnetic ball from pocket, display in left hand.

SAY: "I'll put the ball in the cup properly this time."

DO: Cover magnetic ball with cup (right hand). With left hand, pretend to scoop ball out from under cup and deposit it into left jacket pocket -- actually picking up the ordinary miniball instead.

DO: Produce ordinary miniball from pocket and display it. (Audience believes this is the ball they just watched go in.)

DO: Cover ordinary miniball with cup. With closed left hand, actually scoop it out. Pause. Open left hand to show the ball is genuinely there -- you really took it.

SAY: "I really took it. Look."

DO: Deposit ordinary miniball into left jacket pocket. Remove closed hand slowly. Pause. Open -- empty.

SAY: "And now it's gone."

NOTE: The audience is now confused about which ball is which. Good. That's the point.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 3: THE BANG (convincer for those who know the chop cup)
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Show left hand open and flat. Right hand holds cup face-down above it.

SAY: "For those of you who think you know what's happening -- watch."

DO: Bang cup firmly down onto left palm. This is a deliberate convincer: the sharp impact would dislodge any loosely held magnetic ball, "proving" there's nothing inside. Lift cup. Show nothing underneath.

NOTE: The chop ball IS still held magnetically -- applied pressure during the bang keeps it secure. This is the moment that should confuse Penn and Teller most. They'll clock the bang and think: OK, nothing's there. But then:

DO: Apply deliberate pressure with right hand to dislodge ball from gimmick. Ball settles onto left palm under the cup.

SAY: (to someone nearby) "Whenever you're ready, we'll begin."

DO: Lift cup. Magnetic miniball is sitting there on the left palm.

SAY: "So. Let's start."

NOTE: Pause here. Expect a reaction. If applause, accept it briefly. If not, move on -- Don Alan style: act like nothing extraordinary has happened.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 4: THE TELEPORT TO SPECTATOR'S HAND
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Ball in open left hand. Cover with cup using right hand.

SAY: "This time I'm going to move the ball to your hand. Not your pocket. Your hand."

DO: Mime sweeping ball out from under cup with closed left hand. Pretend to deposit into left jacket pocket.

SAY: "I only pretend to put it in my pocket -- it's still here."

DO: Open left hand and show ball is still there.

SAY: (to spectator) "Open your hand. Cup it like a bowl."

DO: Cover ball with cup again. With left hand, mime scooping ball out from under cup, deposit (nothing) into left jacket pocket. Left hand actually retrieves a miniball from jacket pocket.

DO: Bring closed left hand out of pocket. Pause. Open -- ball is there.

DO: Place ball on top of overturned cup in right hand.

SAY: "Your hand."

DO: Slap down with left hand on ordinary miniball (non-magnetic). Simultaneously dislodge magnetic ball from gimmick with right hand. Magnetic ball drops into spectator's cupped hand.

DO: Left hand finger-palms ordinary ball. Right hand repositions cup face-down over finger-palmed ball in left hand.

NOTE: Cup is now loaded with chop ball inside AND ordinary miniball under it in left hand. Audience believes spectator just caught the ball from the cup.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 5: FIRST BIG LOAD -- GREEN TENNIS BALL
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Deposit chop ball and Hecklau gimmick into right pants pocket. Immediately grasp green tennis ball.

DO: One-handed flourish -- turn cup right-side-up with fingers on top. This produces a sound that suggests something is inside.

SAY: "Give that to me for a second."

DO: Ask spectator to tip ball from their hand back. Dump the ball from the cup (which has been loaded) into spectator's hand using the face-down cup. Right hand, with tennis ball in front of body, brings hands together to transfer cup from left to right hand. Relax right hand. Swing slightly outward. Left hand grabs the miniball.

SAY: "Wait."

DO: With left hand, place miniball on top of overturned cup in right hand (which has tennis ball inside).

SAY: "One ball. Small ball. Let me put it away."

DO: With left hand, openly take miniball off top of cup. Deposit into left pants pocket. Immediately grasp pink tennis ball (2nd big load).

SAY: "Open your hand."

DO: Release first tennis ball (green) from cup into spectator's hand. Raise cup to eye level to show empty. Remove pink tennis ball from pocket. Bring hands together -- load pink tennis ball into cup. Relax left hand. Swing slightly outward.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 6: SECOND BIG LOAD -- PINK TENNIS BALL
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Take green tennis ball from spectator with right hand.

SAY: "A ball this big can't go through the bottom of the cup."

DO: Show that it doesn't. Place green tennis ball into right jacket pocket. Grasp giant foil ball (3rd load).

SAY: "Open your hand."

DO: Release pink tennis ball (pink) from cup. Raise to eye level -- empty. Remove giant foil ball from pocket. Transfer cup from left to right hand, loading silver ball. Relax left hand.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 7: THIRD BIG LOAD -- GIANT SILVER FOIL BALL
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Take pink tennis ball from spectator with left hand.

SAY: "Two balls definitely won't fit in this cup. You can feel how small it is."

DO: Show it can't fit. Place pink tennis ball into left jacket pocket. Immediately grasp lemon.

SAY: "Now. The silver ball. Where did the silver ball go? I'll be honest -- I don't know either. But maybe we can ask."

DO: Pause for effect.

SAY: (to silver ball, theatrically) "Where are you?"

SAY: (to spectator) "Open your hand."

DO: Relax right hand -- silver giant foil ball drops into spectator's hand. Meanwhile remove lemon from left jacket pocket. Show cup empty at eye level. Transfer cup from right to left hand, loading lemon securely.

------------------------------------------------------
PHASE 8: FOURTH AND FINAL LOAD -- THE LEMON
------------------------------------------------------

DO: Take silver ball from spectator with right hand.

SAY: "And just to be completely clear -- the silver ball also cannot go through the bottom of this cup."

DO: Show it can't.

SAY: "The cup is too small. Obviously."

DO: Pause.

SAY: "Which makes this --"

DO: Slowly straighten left hand fully. Overturn cup. Lemon falls out.

SAY: "-- awkward."

NOTE: Hold for reaction. Don't talk. Let it land.

------------------------------------------------------
RUNNING TIME: approx. 5-6 minutes
