# Yishai — Show Structure & Repertoire

## Character

**Archetype: The Jester** — a funny, warm guy who happens to be able to read your mind. The comedy comes first, the impossible moments land harder because nobody was bracing for them. The audience is laughing, relaxed, guard down — then something genuinely impossible happens and it hits like a truck.

**One-liner:** "I'm not a serious mentalist. I'm a comedian who accidentally became psychic."

**Consistency rule (per Henning Nelms / Drew Backenstoss):** Everything — walk-on, patter, reactions to volunteers, handling mistakes — should be filtered through the Jester lens. If something goes wrong, it's funny. If something goes right, it's funny AND impossible.

## 10-Step Framework (Drew Backenstoss)

### Step 1: Character = Jester (see above)

### Step 2: Finish with a BANG, work backwards
- **Closer = Cryptext** (always). Multiple reveals, personal to client, standing ovation.
- Everything before it builds toward this moment.

### Step 3: Core + Spine
- **Core effect:** Name and Place — it's your funniest, most interactive, most "you" piece. This is the heart of the show.
- **Spine:** The thread of comedy. Each effect has a comedic wrapper. Callbacks to earlier jokes tie the show together as a unified experience, not a series of tricks.

### Step 4: Get Personal
- Phone Unlock uses THEIR phone
- Cryptext uses THEIR money and the CLIENT'S name
- PK Touch uses THEIR body
- Name and Place uses THEIR thoughts
- Every effect touches something personal to the spectator

### Step 5: Involve the Audience
- Book Test: multiple people choose page/line/word, whole audience shouts letters
- Magic Square: audience chooses the number
- Ultimate Vacation: multiple people call out info on the spot
- Egg beater gag: volunteer physically involved in a ridiculous way

### Step 6: Flow and Build
- Build from funny-and-clever → funny-and-impossible → funny-and-devastating
- Vary the staging: start in the audience, move to stage, bring people up
- Plant seeds early (e.g., something set up in effect #2 pays off in the closer)
- Layer climaxes at the end (Drew's favorite strategy)

### Step 7: Contingencies
- If a volunteer doesn't want to participate: Jester character makes it easy — "No worries, I'll read the mind of someone braver" (gets a laugh, moves on)
- If a reveal misses: comedy covers — "See, I told you I wasn't serious about this"

## Stage Show (45 min)

**The build order follows these principles:**
1. Each effect is more impressive than the last
2. Comedy peaks in the middle (Name and Place), impact peaks at the end (Cryptext)
3. Variety in type: prediction → physical → mind reading → mathematical → devastating → impossible

### ACT 1 — "Let me show you what I do" (0-12 min)
*Establish the Jester character. Get laughs early. Show credibility without being heavy.*

**Effect 1: Ultimate Vacation (5 min)**
- Opens fast, no setup, audience calls out info, instant sealed prediction
- Tone: light, playful, "let's have some fun"
- Establishes: I can predict things

**Effect 2: White Dwarf (7 min)**
- Deeper than Ultimate Vacation — they write something personal, sealed in envelopes, you divine it
- Escalation: now it's not just random info, it's something THEY chose privately
- Establishes: I can read your private thoughts

### ACT 2 — "Now let's get weird" (12-30 min)
*This is the comedy engine of the show. Maximum audience interaction, maximum laughs, maximum byplay.*

**Effect 3: Name and Place + Drawing Duplication (12 min)**
- YOUR CORE EFFECT — baby gag, egg beater gag, billet burn drama
- This is where the Jester character shines brightest
- The audience is laughing the hardest here
- But the reveals are genuinely impossible — dual revelation + drawing
- Establishes: I can read minds AND I'm hilarious

**Effect 4: Book Test (8 min)**
- Multiple audience members involved (page, line, word)
- Whole audience participates in the anagram scramble
- Interactive, playful, builds energy
- Establishes: the whole room is part of this

**Effect 5: PK Touch (8 min)**
- SHIFT IN TONE — still warm and funny (volunteer reactions) but now it's visceral and physical
- The audience goes quiet when they see the second person reacting in real time
- This is the pivot from "funny show" to "wait... is this real?"
- Establishes: this isn't tricks — something genuinely strange is happening

### ACT 3 — "I told you I wasn't serious" (30-45 min)
*The comedy has earned you their trust. Now deliver the impossible. Layer the climaxes.*

**Effect 6: Card/Number Sequence — Invisible Deck → Magic Square → ACAAN (10 min)**
- Thought-of card revealed as reversed (gasp)
- Magic Square filled live — every combination adds to their number (intellectual wow)
- Same card at same number in the mem deck (triple payoff)
- Establishes: mathematical precision + impossibility

**Effect 7: Phone Unlock (3 min)**
- Short, devastating, real
- The audience reaction is the comedy — you don't need jokes, their faces do the work
- Transition moment: "OK now I'm actually scared of this guy"

**Effect 8: Cryptext — THE CLOSER (7 min)**
- Borrowed bill, multiplied numbers, poster reveals matching decimal
- Bill number matches
- Poster flipped: CLIENT'S NAME
- Standing ovation moment
- Brings the show full circle — you started light and funny, you end with something that feels like a miracle

## Walk-Around / Restaurant (2-4 min per table)

Rotate these sets so nearby tables see different effects:

| Set | Effect | Best for |
|-----|--------|----------|
| 1 | White Dwarf | Any table — versatile, anything they write |
| 2 | Ultimate Vacation | Quick hit, multiple people at the table |
| 3 | PK Touch | Couples, groups of friends — physical, memorable |
| 4 | Phone Unlock | High-energy tables, young crowd |
| 5 | Name and Place (short version, no gags) | When you have a bit more time |

**Restaurant-specific notes:**
- Keep it 2-4 minutes max per table
- Save the comedy gags (baby, egg beater) for stage — at a table they slow you down
- White Dwarf and Ultimate Vacation are your workhorses
- Phone Unlock is your reputation-maker — use selectively

---

## Corporate Reception / Networking (roaming, 3-5 min per group)

- Ultimate Vacation
- White Dwarf  
- Phone Unlock
- PK Touch

Short, punchy, conversation-starting pieces. The comedy comes from your personality and byplay, not built-in gags.

## Callbacks & Spine Ideas

- Reference the egg beater later in the show: "Don't worry, I left the egg beater backstage for this one"
- If someone's White Dwarf revelation was funny (e.g., they wrote something embarrassing), call back to it during a later effect
- During Cryptext, when revealing the client's name: "See? I knew your name the whole time. I just wanted to make you sit through 45 minutes first."

---

## Key Rules

1. **Cryptext is ALWAYS the closer** — most theatrical, most personal, most impossible
2. **Name and Place is ALWAYS the core** — funniest, most "you," heart of the show
3. **PK Touch is the pivot** — the moment the show shifts from comedy to "this is real"
4. **Each effect must be more impressive than the last** — never go backwards
5. **The Jester earns the right to be impossible** — they laugh first, then they believe
6. **Plant seeds, reap at the end** — Drew's layered climax strategy
7. **For restaurants: speed and impact over comedy** — save the gags for stage
