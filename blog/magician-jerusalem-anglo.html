By Yishai Rasowsky  ·  April 2026  ·  4 min read

Jerusalem has one of the most diverse and vibrant Anglo communities in Israel — Anglos in Ramot, in Har Nof, in Katamon and the German Colony, in Rechavia, in Ramat Eshkol. Different backgrounds, different levels of observance, but a shared thread: English-speaking families building their lives in the most meaningful city in the world.

I'm Yishai Rasowsky — a magician and mentalist who performs regularly in Jerusalem for exactly this community. Here's what that looks like.

Jerusalem Is a Unique Crowd

Jerusalem Anglo families come from everywhere. You might have family visiting from the US sitting next to Israeli relatives who don't speak a word of English. You have academics, rabbis, young professionals, Har Nof families, and Katamon families — sometimes all in the same room. The show needs to work for all of them, and I've performed in enough Jerusalem living rooms and halls to know how to read that crowd.

English is my first language. For an Anglo crowd the show runs primarily in English, with Hebrew woven in naturally for the Israelis in the room. Both communities feel included — nobody is mentally translating anything.

Events I Do Most Often in Jerusalem

Bar and Bat Mitzvahs

The bar mitzvah boy becomes the star. I design moments specifically around him — his personality, his friends, his family — so that by the end, everyone who loves him has laughed together and been genuinely amazed. It's a different kind of tribute than a speech, and it lands differently because of that.

Sheva Berachos

Jerusalem sheva berachos often have the most wonderfully mixed crowds — family from abroad, Israeli friends, colleagues, neighbors. The intimacy of the format is perfect for magic and mentalism. The couple gets a moment both sides of the family will remember, and the room comes together around it.

Shul and Community Events

Jerusalem has no shortage of shul events, yeshiva dinners, school galas, and community evenings. I've performed at Avos U'Banim events, women's charity evenings, school end-of-year celebrations, and melaveh malkahs. The show fits naturally in a religious setting — clean, engaging, and appropriate for every age.

Chanukah and Purim Parties

There's no better city than Jerusalem to celebrate Chanukah and Purim — and few better formats than magic for bringing a room of all ages together in pure joy. Community parties, neighborhood gatherings, school events — I perform at all of them.

A Note on Appropriateness

The show is completely clean and family-appropriate, and works naturally in a religious setting regardless of the level of observance in the room. Nothing edgy, nothing that requires explanation. If you have specific requirements — timing around davening, seating arrangements, anything else — I work around them without making it a production.
