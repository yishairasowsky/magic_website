Looking for a Magician in Ramat Bet Shemesh?

By Yishai Rasowsky  ·  April 2026  ·  4 min read

Ramat Bet Shemesh is one of the largest English-speaking Torah communities in Israel. Which means when a family in RBS is planning a bar mitzvah, a sheva berachos, or a Purim party — they want entertainment that doesn't require translation. Not just "available in English," but genuinely, naturally English.

That's what I do. I'm Yishai Rasowsky — a magician and mentalist based nearby in Beitar Illit, and I perform regularly in RBS and the surrounding area. If you've been searching for a performer who actually fits your community, here's what that looks like.

What Makes RBS Different (and Why It Matters for Entertainment)

The RBS crowd is a specific crowd. You've got guests who grew up in New York, Baltimore, London, and Johannesburg — people who appreciate wit, who have seen a lot of entertainment, and who know the difference between something polished and something phoned in. You also have kids who've been to enough shul Purim parties to be a tough audience. And you have zeides and bubbes visiting from abroad who want to understand every word.

The common thread is that everyone expects the show to feel natural — not translated, not stilted, not culturally off. When it's right, the whole room connects. When it's wrong, everyone feels it, even if they can't say exactly why.

I've performed in enough Anglo crowds to know how to read this room. The humor is real, the crowd work is genuine, and the energy builds the way it's supposed to.

The Events I Do Most Often in RBS

Bar Mitzvahs

The bar mitzvah boy is the star of everything I do. I build moments specifically around him — his personality, his friends, the things that make him unique in the room. His family watches something impossible happen through him, and it lands differently than any speech or song. I've done bar mitzvahs across RBS Aleph, Bet, and the newer neighborhoods, and it's consistently one of the most satisfying events to perform at.

Sheva Berachos

Intimate, warm, everyone there because they love the couple. These crowds often have family visiting from abroad mixed with Israeli friends — I navigate the bilingual room naturally, with the show running primarily in English when the crowd calls for it. The couple gets a moment both sides of the family will talk about for years.

Purim Parties

Community and neighborhood Purim parties are some of the most energetic events I perform at. Magic and mentalism hit differently when the whole kehilla is in the room together. Kids and adults, all in costume, all in the best mood of the year — it's a great night.

Shul and School Events

Melaveh malkahs, school gala dinners, yeshiva fundraiser evenings — events where the entertainment needs to be classy, engaging, and completely appropriate. I've performed at enough of these to know exactly what that means and how to deliver it.

Completely Appropriate for a Frum Crowd

This matters, so I'll be direct about it. Everything I do is clean, family-appropriate, and suitable for a frum setting. There's nothing edgy, nothing that requires explanation, nothing that would make anyone in the room uncomfortable. The humor is warm and intelligent. The show works for mixed-gender audiences. If you have specific requirements about timing, seating, or anything else, I work around them — it's not a production.
