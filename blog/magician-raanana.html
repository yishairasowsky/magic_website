Ra'anana has one of the highest concentrations of English speakers in Israel. Thousands of Anglo families have built their lives here -- from the UK, North America, South Africa, Australia -- and the city reflects that in its culture, its schools, and the way it celebrates. When a Ra'anana family makes a simcha, the room is often half-English, half-Hebrew, and entirely expecting something good.

I'm Yishai Rasowsky -- a professional magician and mentalist performing across Israel, including regularly in Ra'anana and the Sharon area. I perform naturally in English and Hebrew. Here's what entertainment for a Ra'anana crowd looks like.

What Ra'anana Events Are Like

Ra'anana crowds are warm, social, and used to quality. The Anglo community here has been around long enough that people know each other across shuls, schools, and neighbourhoods. When you're entertaining at a Ra'anana simcha, you're performing for people who've been to a lot of events -- and they'll notice the difference between a performer who's genuinely connected to the room and one who's just going through the motions.

That's the standard I hold myself to. The crowd work is real, the humour is genuine, and the show builds the way it's supposed to.
