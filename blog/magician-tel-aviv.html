Looking for a Magician in Tel Aviv?

By Yishai Rasowsky  ·  June 2026  ·  4 min read

Tel Aviv is Israel's most international city -- a mix of local Israelis, expats, and visitors from all over the world, all in the same room at the same time. Corporate events, private parties, product launches, wedding celebrations -- the entertainment expectations here are high, and the crowd has seen a lot. That's exactly the kind of challenge I enjoy.

I'm Yishai Rasowsky -- a professional magician and mentalist performing across Israel, including frequently in Tel Aviv. I work in English and Hebrew and perform at everything from intimate private parties to large corporate cocktail hours. Here's what that looks like.

What Works in Tel Aviv

Tel Aviv crowds are sophisticated. They're not easily impressed, and they can smell something generic from across the room. What works here is close-up magic -- performed right in the guests' hands, with real crowd work and genuine improvisation. When something impossible happens to you personally, in your own hands, there's no distance to retreat to. The reaction is honest and it's real.

Mentalism works especially well in a Tel Aviv setting. Mind reading, psychological influence, moments that feel more like an experience than a trick -- these land differently with a crowd that thinks of itself as too smart to be fooled. Which makes it all the better when it happens anyway.

Events I Do Most Often in Tel Aviv

Corporate Events and Cocktail Hours

Product launches, company milestones, conference receptions, team events -- close-up magic is the best entertainment format for corporate cocktail hours because it works in the room, not in front of it. I move through the crowd, perform for small groups, and bring people together who might otherwise stand in separate corners. It works in English, Hebrew, or both.

Private Parties and Celebrations

Birthday parties, anniversaries, engagement parties -- events where the host wants to give guests something they'll actually remember. Close-up magic scales to any size and any vibe, from a small dinner party to a large celebration at one of Tel Aviv's event venues.

Weddings and Receptions

Cocktail hour entertainment at a Tel Aviv wedding is one of the most natural settings for what I do. Guests are mingling, they're in a great mood, and they want something to talk about between courses. I move through the room and give them that.
