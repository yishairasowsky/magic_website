Looking for a Magician in Modi'in?

By Yishai Rasowsky  ·  June 2026  ·  4 min read

Modi'in is one of Israel's most diverse cities -- a mix of native Israelis, Anglo olim, and international professionals who came for the tech companies and stayed for the quality of life. That mix shows up at events. A bar mitzvah in Modi'in might have guests from New York, Tel Aviv, and three different Israeli cities all in the same room.

I'm Yishai Rasowsky -- a magician and mentalist who performs across Israel, including regularly in Modi'in. I work in both English and Hebrew, and I navigate a bilingual room naturally. Here's what entertainment for a Modi'in crowd actually looks like.

The Modi'in Crowd

Modi'in tends to be educated, curious, and a little harder to fool than average -- which I take as a challenge I enjoy. The tech professionals, the academics, the people who think they know how things work -- they're the most satisfying crowds to perform for, because when something impossible happens right in their hands, there's nowhere to hide. The reaction is genuine.

At the same time, Modi'in events are warm. Community-oriented. Whether it's a bar mitzvah at one of the local halls or a corporate team day at one of the nearby office parks, the people in the room want to have a good time together.

Events I Do Most Often in Modi'in

Bar and Bat Mitzvahs

The bar or bat mitzvah kid is the star of everything I build. I create moments specifically around them -- their personality, their friends, the things that make them memorable in the room. The family watches something impossible happen through their child. I've performed at bar mitzvahs in Modi'in for both Anglo and Israeli families, and the format works for both.

Corporate Events and Team Days

Modi'in has a significant tech and business presence. Close-up magic and mentalism work exceptionally well for corporate events -- whether it's a team-building day, a company milestone celebration, or cocktail hour entertainment at a conference. It works in both Hebrew and English, and it gets people talking to each other in a way that no other entertainment format does.

Birthday Parties and Private Events

Milestone birthdays, family gatherings, private parties -- I perform close-up for small groups at a time, so every guest gets a personal experience rather than watching from a distance. It works for ages 8 to 80.

Bilingual Without Making It a Thing

A lot of Modi'in events have both Hebrew and English speakers in the room. I don't make a production of switching between languages -- I just do it, naturally, wherever the crowd calls for it. Nobody feels like they're getting the B-version of the show.
