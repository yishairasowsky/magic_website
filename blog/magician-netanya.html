Looking for a Magician in Netanya?

By Yishai Rasowsky  ·  June 2026  ·  4 min read

Netanya has long been home to one of Israel's most international communities -- French, English, South African, and American families who chose this coastal city and built a life here. When it's time to celebrate, the room reflects that. A birthday party or bar mitzvah in Netanya often has guests from multiple countries, multiple languages, and very different frames of reference for what "entertainment" means.

I'm Yishai Rasowsky -- a magician and mentalist based in Israel, performing across the country including regularly in Netanya. I work in English, Hebrew, and can navigate a bilingual room without making it awkward. Here's what entertainment for a Netanya crowd actually looks like.

The Netanya Crowd

Netanya events tend to be warm and social -- people who've been neighbours and friends for years, international guests visiting from abroad, a mix of generations from kids to grandparents. Close-up magic and mentalism work perfectly in this setting because they're personal. I'm not performing at the front of a room while people watch. I'm right there with the guests -- in their hands, in their space, making something impossible happen directly for them.

Events I Do Most Often in Netanya

Birthday Parties

Milestone birthdays -- 50ths, 60ths, 70ths -- are one of the best formats for close-up magic. Everyone in the room is there because they love the person celebrating. I move through the crowd, performing for small groups at a time, and by the end the whole room has had a personal experience they'll be talking about long after the cake is gone.

Bar and Bat Mitzvahs

The bar or bat mitzvah kid is the star of everything I build. Their personality, their friends, their moment -- I create something specifically for them that their entire family, from every country in the room, will remember. The show runs in whatever language or combination of languages fits the crowd.

Private Events and Family Gatherings

Reunions, anniversaries, holiday parties -- events where the intimacy of the group is the whole point. Close-up magic scales perfectly to these settings. The smaller and more personal the group, the more powerful the experience.

Multiple Languages, One Show

Netanya crowds are often genuinely multilingual. I work in English and Hebrew naturally, without making a production of switching. Everyone in the room gets the same experience -- nobody is watching a translated version of the show.
