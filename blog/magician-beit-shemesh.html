Looking for a Magician in Beit Shemesh?

By Yishai Rasowsky  ·  June 2026  ·  4 min read

Beit Shemesh has become one of the largest Anglo communities in Israel. Between the older neighbourhoods and the newer areas of Ramat Beit Shemesh, you have tens of thousands of English-speaking families celebrating bar mitzvahs, birthdays, weddings, and everything in between. When it's time to add entertainment to your event, the last thing you want is a language barrier in the middle of the room.

I'm Yishai Rasowsky — a magician and mentalist based nearby in Beitar Illit. I perform regularly across Beit Shemesh and RBS, and I perform naturally in English. Here's what that looks like in practice.

Why "English-Speaking" Actually Matters

There's a difference between a performer who can get by in English and one who thinks, improvises, and does crowd work in English. The humour lands differently. The connection with guests is real. When your Uncle from New York and your grandmother from London are in the room, and the performer is genuinely one of them — everyone feels it.

That's what I bring to events in Beit Shemesh. It's not a feature, it's just how I work.

Events I Do Most Often in Beit Shemesh

Bar and Bat Mitzvahs

The bar or bat mitzvah kid is the centre of everything I do. I build moments specifically around them — their personality, their friends, what makes them unique in the room. Their family watches something impossible happen through them. I've performed at bar mitzvahs across Beit Shemesh and RBS and it's consistently one of the most rewarding events to perform at.

Birthday Parties

Whether it's a milestone 40th, a 60th with the whole family, or a kids' party where you want something genuinely memorable — close-up magic and mentalism work for every age and every size. I move through the room, performing for small groups at a time, so everyone gets a personal experience rather than just watching from the back.

Sheva Berachos

Intimate, warm, everyone there because they love the couple. I build the show around the chasan and kallah — creating something impossible through both of them — while making the whole room feel included. It's one of the most satisfying formats I work in.

Community and School Events

Melaveh malkahs, school galas, shul Purim parties — events where the entertainment needs to be engaging, age-appropriate, and completely suitable for a frum crowd. I've done enough of these to know exactly what that means.
