Looking for a Magician in Ma'ale Adumim?

By Yishai Rasowsky  ·  June 2026  ·  4 min read

Ma'ale Adumim is a close-knit community just outside Jerusalem -- a mix of long-time Israeli residents and Anglo families who chose a quieter, more community-oriented alternative to the city. The social bonds here are tight. People know each other through shul, through school, through years of living next door. When a family makes a simcha, the whole community feels it.

I'm Yishai Rasowsky -- a magician and mentalist based nearby in Beitar Illit. I perform across the greater Jerusalem area including in Ma'ale Adumim, in both English and Hebrew. Here's what entertainment for your community looks like.

The Ma'ale Adumim Feel

Events in Ma'ale Adumim have a warmth that's hard to find in bigger cities. The guests aren't strangers -- they've shared Shabbos tables, they've watched each other's kids grow up, they know the bar mitzvah boy's whole story. That warmth is the best possible foundation for what I do. When I build a show around the person being celebrated, and the whole room already loves that person, the moments land deeper.

The crowd tends to be genuinely bilingual -- Israeli families alongside Anglo ones, with visiting relatives from abroad adding to the mix. I navigate that naturally, in real time, without making it an issue.

Events I Do Most Often in Ma'ale Adumim

Bar and Bat Mitzvahs

This is one of the most satisfying events I do in a community like Ma'ale Adumim. The bar mitzvah boy is surrounded by people who have known him his whole life -- which means when I build moments specifically around him, those moments land with extra weight. His friends are in stitches. His parents are emotional. The grandparents visiting from abroad are stunned. Everyone leaves talking about the same thing.

Birthday Parties and Family Celebrations

Milestone birthdays, family reunions, anniversary celebrations -- close-up magic scales perfectly to the intimate, warm format of a Ma'ale Adumim family event. I perform for small groups at a time, so everyone gets a personal experience rather than watching from a distance.

Community and Shul Events

Melaveh malkahs, shul dinners, school events -- entertainment that needs to be appropriate, engaging, and genuinely enjoyable for every age in the room. I've performed at enough of these to know exactly how to pitch it right.
